Normal Subgroup, Quotient Group, Isomorphism Theorems

Normal Subgroup

A subgroup \( N \) of a group \( G \) is called a normal subgroup if it is invariant under conjugation by elements of \( G \). That is, \( N \) is normal in \( G \) if for all \( g \in G \) and \( n \in N \), the element \( gng^{-1} \in N \). This is denoted as \( N \triangleleft G \).

Quotient Group

If \( N \) is a normal subgroup of \( G \), the quotient group \( G/N \) is defined as the set of left cosets of \( N \) in \( G \) with the operation \((aN)(bN) = (ab)N\). The elements of \( G/N \) are of the form \( gN \) where \( g \in G \), and the operation is well-defined due to the normality of \( N \).

Isomorphism Theorems

First Isomorphism Theorem

Let \( \varphi: G \to H \) be a group homomorphism. Then the kernel of \( \varphi \), \( \ker(\varphi) \), is a normal subgroup of \( G \), and the image of \( \varphi \), \( \mathrm{Im}(\varphi) \), is a subgroup of \( H \). Furthermore, \( G/\ker(\varphi) \cong \mathrm{Im}(\varphi) \).

\[ G/\ker(\varphi) \cong \mathrm{Im}(\varphi) \]

Second Isomorphism Theorem

Let \( G \) be a group, and let \( A \) and \( B \) be subgroups of \( G \) with \( B \) normal in \( G \). Then \( AB \) is a subgroup of \( G \), \( B \) is a normal subgroup of \( AB \), and \((AB)/B \cong A/(A \cap B)\).

\[ (AB)/B \cong A/(A \cap B) \]

Third Isomorphism Theorem

Let \( G \) be a group with \( N \) and \( M \) as normal subgroups of \( G \) such that \( N \subseteq M \subseteq G \). Then \( M/N \) is a normal subgroup of \( G/N \) and \((G/N)/(M/N) \cong G/M \).

\[ (G/N)/(M/N) \cong G/M \]
